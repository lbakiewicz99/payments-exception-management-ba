# Current State Process

## Summary

The current payment exception handling process is manual, fragmented, and dependent on spreadsheets, email communication, and analyst judgement.

There is no single source of truth for exception ownership, status, SLA, escalation, and closure evidence.

## Current process steps

| Step | Activity                          | Owner               | Tool / Source                                | Pain point                             |
|------|-----------------------------------|---------------------|----------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| 1    | Payment exception is identified   | Analyst             | Bank file, PSP report, payment system, email | Exceptions come from multiple sources  |
| 2    | Analyst reviews exception details | Analyst             | Payment system / spreadsheet                 | Manual lookup required                 |
| 3    | Exception is added to spreadsheet | Analyst             | Excel / shared file                          | Inconsistent data entry                |
| 4    | Priority is assigned manually     | Analyst             | Spreadsheet                                  | No standard priority rules             |
| 5    | Owner is assigned manually        | Team lead / Analyst | Email / spreadsheet                          | Ownership may be unclear               |
| 6    | Investigation is performed        | Analyst             | Payment system, bank portal, PSP portal      | Notes are not always standardized      |
| 7    | Follow-up is sent                 | Analyst             | Email / Teams                                | Follow-up history is fragmented        |
| 8    | Status is updated                 | Analyst             | Spreadsheet                                  | Status values may be inconsistent      |
| 9    | Escalation is performed if needed | Analyst / Team lead | Email                                        | Escalation rules are not standardized  |
| 10   | Exception is closed               | Analyst             | Spreadsheet                                  | Closure evidence may be incomplete     |
| 11   | Management report is prepared     | Team lead           | Excel / manual summary                       | Reporting is manual and time-consuming |

## Key issues

* No single source of truth for open exceptions.
* Status values are not standarized.
* SLA and aging are calculated manually.
* Escalation depend on manual review.
* Management reporting requires manual effort.
* Investigation notes and closure reasons are inconsistent.
* Audit trail is limited.
